What to expect during migration
Migration will require downtime of your GCSv4 endpoint. At a high level, migration involves first installing and configuring a new GCSv5 endpoint and then cutting over to the GCSv5 endpoint by migrating the v4 endpoint ID to the v5 endpoint. When the endpoint ID is migrated, all in-flight and pending transfers to and from your GCSv4 endpoint will be canceled. In-flight and pending transfers, as well as pause rules, will not resume after migration.

What to expect after migration
After migration, your users should see no change. Task histories and bookmarks will be preserved. Continuity of metrics in your subscriber usage reports should be maintained. However, applications that access your endpoint may need to be updated to support GCSv5. A guide for adding application support for GCSv5 can be found here:
https://docs.globus.org/globus-connect-server/migrating-to-v5.4/application-migration/

Please contact us at support@globus.org when you begin migration of your endpoint(s) or if you have any questions.
